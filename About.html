I'm Thomas — a product designer based in London, drawn to the kind of work where the complexity is the point. Outside of work I'm usually chasing sun somewhere and asking too many questions about how things are made.

Background

My background in engineering meant learning to think in systems, work within hard constraints, and asking why something fails before asking how to fix it. That foundation shapes everything about how I approach a problem. I'm less drawn to the aesthetic problem first but more the to the structural one. How does the data model support this decision? Where does the user's mental model break down? It trained me to find the root cause not just describe the symptom.

Good design is rarely talked about, whereas bad design always is. The goal is to make something so considered that nobody notices the decisions that went into it.

Most of my career has been inside complex B2B products - ESG platforms, data pipelines, enterprise SaaS where the problems are genuinely hard and the users have no patience for things that don't work. I don't shy away from technology to help accelerate my process but I use them to get to the interesting decisions faster, not to skip the thinking.

Outside of work, I'm building two startups — one in the creator economy, one in local trades and services. Both are early, both are keeping me honest about what it actually takes to ship something from scratch.
